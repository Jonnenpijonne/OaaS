# Escalation Model

## Purpose

This document defines how operational issues are escalated when normal handling is not enough.

Escalation removes ambiguity around when to involve technical owners, service owners or business stakeholders.

---

## Escalation triggers

Escalate when:

- user or business impact increases
- the issue affects service availability
- recovery path is unclear
- ownership is unclear
- a change caused unexpected impact
- the issue repeats
- evidence suggests a security or compliance concern
- communication to stakeholders is needed

---

## Escalation levels

| Level | Situation | Escalation target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| L1 | Routine operational issue | Operations owner |
| L2 | Technical analysis or recovery needed | Technical owner |
| L3 | Service impact, priority or customer impact | Service owner |
| L4 | Security, compliance or continuity concern | Management / governance owner |

---

## Escalation record

An escalation should record:

- issue summary
- current impact
- owner before escalation
- escalation reason
- new owner or decision maker
- next action
- communication need
- evidence link

---

## Key rule

Escalation is not failure. Escalation is how operational risk becomes visible before it becomes unmanaged risk.
